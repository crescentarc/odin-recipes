Skirt Steak

Image Source

This tutorial, which I learned through the YouTube cooking series Binging with Babish, teaches how to cook thinner cuts of beef. It also provides instructions on marinating the beef with spices like salt and pepper prior to cooking. Cuts like strip steak tend to be on the inexpensive side, so this tutorial is good for your average late night dinner. These cuts of steak don't require an oven, so it's less time and labor consuming to cook.

Other points to keep in mind are as follows: At the end, there are steps to let the steak rest before cutting. from what I've researched, this is to ensure that the steak maintain it's juicy flavor. Additionally, the cutting process involves cutting perpendicular of muscle fibers, which are those visible, vertical lines running across the meat. Cutting perpendicular to those lines ensures that the steak is tender and soft to the bite.

Ingredients

  • Thin cut steak (i.e. skirt steak)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Kosher salt
  • Grinded black pepper (ideally freshly ground)

Equipment

  • Stainless steel or cast iron pan
  • Instant read thermometer
  • Sharp knife (i.e. chef's knife)
  • Aluminum foil sheet
  • Cutting board

Steps

  1. Start by seasoning the steaks generously with kosher salt and black pepper, but only on one side of the steak.
  2. Heat 1 (or 2) Tbsp of vegetable oil over medium high heat until smoking.
  3. Lay steaks seasoned side down onto pan slowly to prevent splattering.
  4. Sear the steaks, flipping once golden brown. Cook until an internal temperature of 115 Degrees (Farenheit).
  5. Rest for ten minutes under foil
  6. After the steaks have rested for 10 minutes, they are ready to carve.
  7. Cut each steak into two halves.
  8. Cut perpendicular to the muscle fibers that run the length of the steak at an angle.
